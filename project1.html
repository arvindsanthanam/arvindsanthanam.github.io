Context

Time management is an issue that all students face; Especially high schoolers when moving to college.

When it comes to completing schoolwork, students’ methods are as diverse as the individuals themselves. Many students, however, share a common obstacle of procrastination and lack of productivity. We set out to better understand the problem with the goal of designing a solution to better help them manage their schedules when they do get to work.

Design Solution

We decided that the most useful design solution is a self-learning time management application where students continually keeps clock-in how long they take on their regular assignments from the start the semester to have the app steadily learn/calculate the student’s assignment completion time and give recommended schedules as to when the student should work on assignments based on his/her schedule.

Interactive Prototype   


Why?

After considering a handful of diverse ideas, our chosen design excels the others primarily due to a smaller budget to build, making it more economically feasible, and is easily accessible through student’s current smartphones, making it socially feasible.


The Process

"When in doubt, Communicate"

Domain exploration and Data collection:
The design process began with observations and interviews which explored student’s work habits; how they preferred working, what distracted them and what changes they’d expected in themselves.

Concept Modeling:
The gamut of ideas received were appropriately sorted based on similarity of thoughts through affinity and empathy mapping, for a better understanding of key problem, student’s intentions and the target audience.


Alan Thomas

Male, 20, American

Student - Sophomore year

Alan is currently a student at Indiana University majoring in accounting. He has 5 classes to attend and works part-time on the weekend.

"I procrastinate a lot, there are plenty of things I want to do at home so I often go to the library to study alone and avoid distraction. I've tried to use a schedule before but it doesn't seem to work perfectly for me at home."

Morgan Walters

Female, 19, American

Student - Freshman year

Morgan is currently doing her first-year student at Indiana University and is majoring in Occupational Therapy. She has 5 classes and occasionally babysits for extra cash.

"I was pretty organized in high school but it's difficult for me now to estimate how long each of my assignment is going to take. I get nervous and my schedule gets messed up pretty often"


Ideation:
With the process of persona development and brainstorming of up to 150 design ideas, we chose to explore 3 ideal solutions

  • VR Study Environment
  • Vibrating Reminder Wristband
  • Self-Learning Time-Management App

Further analysis, including sketching and storyboarding, revealed that the self-learning time-management application showed best potential as a solution.





Prototype Design:
After conducting an internal heuristic and cognitive walkthrough over the lo-fi prototype, issues were fixed in a hi-fidelity prototype to have it tested by users over scenarios using the think-aloud protocol to indicate final changes to the design.

Context

PactSafe is a Business-to-Business software platform designed to protect companies from risk around terms of service in applications and websites. Their main selling point is being able to easily create, update, send, and track terms of service contracts online.

The usability study was conducted over the PactSafe web application to study how people use PactSafe,how effective the interface is, and finally provide suggestions to improve the usability,and the user-experience of the product.


The Process

Market Research:
PactSafe was relatively new to the market and was therefore not well known. It did not feature robust functionality as some of the closest competitors such as Docusign (https://www.docusign.com), Shakelaw (https://www.shakelaw.com), butPactSafe’sidea was novel enough for it to stand out from the others. While all three applications offer the functionality to create, edit and send a contract, few functionalities PactSafe offers to manage contracts, such as the functionality to get details over the interactions made by the signers, and the option to create updated versions of the contract were missing from the other applications.

Market research was followed by Heuristic, Cognitive and Contextual evaluation to elaborate aspects of the design which were pleasing and those which created usability issues.

Heuristic Evaluation:
The heuristic evaluation aggregatedtwenty usability problems based on research and Nielsen’s ten heuristics. These usability problems were thought through from an overall usability impact perspectiveand a prioritized based on the severity of the issues.


Cognitive Walkthrough:
The cognitive walkthrough took a user-centered approach as potential users were identified and user scenarios were created to contextualize how the system would be used. The scenarios were

  • HR Manager: Onboarding paperwork at Pied Piper
  • Website Manager: Updating terms and services
  • Legal counsel: Creating a new version of an existing contract

Representative tasks were identified and a diagrammatic Hierarchical-task analysis tree was created to define the steps a user would take. These tasks were executed in the manner potential users would perform to empathize and understand the target user’s perspective when using the system and to gather data around potential usability issuesin each task.

Contextual inquiry and interviews:
Contextual inquiryinvolved 4 observations and interviews of users after they were asked to perform representative tasks from which the websites usability could be gleaned. In the interviews, the users were asked questions about how the felt along the process of completing the tasks in the application. The sessions captured how new users usethe system, where they struggle, and what they like about the system.

There were some common themes found from the participants in our study.

  • Users were confused about icons as navigational elements. The lack of icon descriptions made navigation challenging for users.
  • Inconsistency of navigational elements confused the users. The “Next” button appeared inconsistently and in unconventional places throughout the platform, making users pause to figure out navigation.
  • There was terminology in the system that wasn’t completely understood by the users. Users were unsure of the definition of some terms, including more technical terms, which made understanding the intended functionality of the system challenging.

Further study by means of think-aloud and interviews were conductedbased on the above themeswith 10 participants of the 3 persona typesby having them complete tasks and answering a series of research questions for us to assimilate qualitative and quantitative data that can help in providing the final design recommendations. Each session with a participant was split into 4; Introduction to the study, participant background Interview, task execution and post-test debriefing. The post-test Debriefing questions consisted of qualitative data, where participants were asked to rate their experience from 0 – 5, and qualitative data regarding how they felt while using the system.

Data Analysis:
Qualitative results to all the research questions were collected and processed through excel sheets to create graphs which would be compared with the qualitative data received to understand the complications faced by the user.

Recommendations


Few of the primary recommendations provided were the following:

  • Create a functional Home icon to improve navigation freedom for the user
  • Use tooltips to help users understand certain options and features which are specific to this


  • Technical jargon such as‘request’ and‘token’ should be replaced with more user friendly terms for better context
  • Provide Navigational Consistency in terms of “Next” Button by placing the icon only at the bottom of each screen.
